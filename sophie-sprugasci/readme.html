Syncopic Bar - Deconstructive song generator

What happens if I decompose a song and mix its parts? You have a choice: listen to the song in its original composition or decompose it, mix the pieces and create a different song. The composition of any track is created from the assembly of individual elements - notes, chords, sounds - and every single different combination gives rise to a different music track. With this in mind, I expanded the starting segment to a five-second interval and thus decomposed a song into larger intervals. These new elements, which are also mixable units, allow for a new re-composition in an almost unlimited number of possible combinations.

Object

As a starting song, I decided to use an instrumental-only piece of electronic music, with a present and constant base to make the segments more cohesive when randomly mixed. The song chosen is 'Crispy Bacon' by Laurent Garnier, from his second studio album '30'.

Disassembled object

To represent the segment visually, I took a picture of the sound waves from Adobe Audition.

WEB PLATFORM

On opening, there is the song broken down into several segments, initially in the correct order, and the 'shuffle' button at the bottom. By clicking on one of them, you can start the song from that point. By clicking on the 'shuffle' button, the different segments will be randomly shuffled, creating a new order.

PLAY

You can play the song by clicking on one of the segments.

SHUFFLE

The "shuffle" button allows to randomise the single elements of the song, creating a new order.

NEXT

The transition from one segment to another is automatic, as if a new song had been created.

SOUND CONTINUITY

I created a slight anticipation of the next track to make the transition between the two smooth. The audio track has a constant base and a rhythm of 130 bpm; I calculated the interval in bps and anticipated the start of the next track by one beat for the shuffle reproduction and used a smaller one for the correct order.
